TITLE: Form I-797: Types and Functions
SOURCE: U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS)

DOCUMENT TYPES: I-797, I-797A, I-797B, I-797C, I-797D, I-797E, I-797F
TOPICS: notices of action, receipt notices, approvals, requests for evidence, biometrics, interviews, transportation letter, immigration benefits
RETRIEVED: 2026-07-21

CONTENT:
USCIS uses several versions of Form I-797 to communicate with applicants and petitioners or to convey an immigration benefit. Form I-797 is not a form that applicants complete or submit.

Form I-797, Notice of Action
- Issued to communicate the receipt or approval of an application or petition.

Form I-797A, Notice of Action
- Issued to an applicant as a replacement Form I-94.

Form I-797B, Notice of Action
- Issued for the approval of an alien worker petition.

Form I-797C, Notice of Action
- Issued to communicate:
  - Receipt or rejection of applications or petitions.
  - Transfer of files.
  - Fingerprint (biometric) appointments.
  - Interview appointments and rescheduled appointments.
  - Additional actions.
  - Reopening of cases.

Form I-797D
- Accompanies benefit cards.

Form I-797E, Notice of Action
- Issued to request additional evidence.

Form I-797F, Transportation Letter
- Issued overseas to allow applicants to travel.
