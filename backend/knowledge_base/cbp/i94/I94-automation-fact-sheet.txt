Overview

To increase efficiency, reduce operating costs, and streamline the admissions process, U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) has automated Form I-94 at air, sea, and land ports of entry. Paper Forms I-94 are generally no longer issued upon arrival except in limited circumstances.

Travelers who require proof of their admission record, immigration status, alien registration, or employment authorization can retrieve and print their electronic I-94 from the CBP I-94 website or the CBP One mobile application.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Form I-94?

Form I-94 is the U.S. Department of Homeland Security (DHS) arrival/departure record. It is issued electronically to travelers who:
- Are admitted to the United States.
- Adjust status while in the United States.
- Extend their immigration status.
- Require an official record of admission.

Travelers must depart the United States on or before the departure date shown on their I-94.

How does I-94 automation affect travelers?

The automation of Form I-94 does not change a traveler's eligibility to enter the United States.

CBP continues to create an I-94 record for travelers who require one, but it is generated electronically instead of on paper.

If a paper copy is needed, travelers may print it from:
- The CBP I-94 website.
- The CBP One mobile application.

Will travelers receive proof of admission?

Yes.

Travelers receive an electronic I-94 record that can be retrieved online. CBP may also place an admission stamp in the travel document showing:
- Date of admission.
- Class of admission.
- Admit-until date.

How are departures recorded?

Travelers generally do not need to take additional steps when leaving the United States.

Air or Sea Departures
- CBP records departures electronically using carrier manifest information.

Land Departures
- A departure is recorded if the traveler departs by land and re-enters the United States before the I-94 expires.
- Travelers who are not residents of Canada or Mexico and who leave by land without re-entering before expiration should keep evidence of departure.

Examples of acceptable evidence include:
- Passport entry stamps.
- Transportation tickets.
- Pay stubs.
- Receipts.
- Other documentation proving departure.

Travelers entering Canada may request an entry stamp from the Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA). Travelers entering Mexico may request an entry stamp from the Instituto Nacional de Migración (INM).

Travelers issued a paper Form I-94 should surrender it to:
- Their commercial carrier.
- CBSA when entering Canada by land.
- A CBP officer or designated drop box at the port of departure.

Travelers who still possess a paper I-94 after departure should follow CBP instructions for returning it to ensure their departure is properly recorded.

Will CBP still issue paper Forms I-94?

Generally, no.

CBP primarily issues electronic I-94 records.

Paper Forms I-94 may still be issued for certain categories of travelers, including:
- Some asylees.
- Some parolees.
- Other situations where CBP determines a paper form is appropriate.

Refugee I-94 records issued after September 7, 2015, are processed electronically and can be retrieved online.

If a traveler cannot locate their electronic I-94, they should:
- Contact CBP through the CBP Help Center.
- Visit a local CBP Deferred Inspection Site or Port of Entry.

What is the CBP I-94 website?

The CBP I-94 website allows travelers to:
- Retrieve and print their electronic I-94.
- Obtain proof of lawful visitor status.
- View a limited history of U.S. arrivals and departures.
- Check travel compliance.

What is the CBP One mobile application?

CBP One is an official mobile application that provides access to multiple CBP services, including:
- Retrieving electronic I-94 records.
- Accessing I-94-related services available through CBP.

What if a traveler was admitted incorrectly?

Travelers who believe their admission information is incorrect should first verify their I-94 record through:
- The CBP I-94 website.
- The CBP One mobile application.

If the information remains incorrect, they should:
- Visit a CBP Deferred Inspection Site or Port of Entry to request a correction.

If an incorrect Form I-94 was issued by U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS), the traveler should refer to Form I-102 or contact the nearest USCIS office.

Key Benefits of I-94 Automation
- Streamlines the admissions process.
- Reduces paper processing.
- Lowers operating costs.
- Provides online access to admission records.
- Supports verification of immigration status and employment authorization.
- Allows travelers to retrieve travel history electronically.
