Overview

All F and M students studying in the United States must have a Form I-20, "Certificate of Eligibility for Nonimmigrant Student Status."

Once accepted into a Student and Exchange Visitor Program (SEVP)-certified school, students receive a Form I-20 from their Designated School Official (DSO).

Students must also obtain a separate Form I-20 for each eligible dependent who will accompany them to the United States.

The type of Form I-20 issued determines:
- The type of student visa (F-1 or M-1) that may be obtained.
- The immigration status the student must maintain while in the United States.

The student and the DSO must sign the Form I-20. Students under 18 years of age must also have a parent or legal guardian sign the form.

Importance of Form I-20

The Form I-20 is an important immigration document that should be kept safe throughout a student's academic program. It is required for multiple immigration and educational processes.

Paying the I-901 SEVIS Fee

Students must first receive a Form I-20 before paying the mandatory I-901 Student and Exchange Visitor Information System (SEVIS) Fee.

Information required to pay the fee includes:
- Full name.
- Address.
- Date of birth.
- Email address.
- Country of birth.
- Country of citizenship.
- School Code listed on the Form I-20.
- SEVIS Identification Number listed on the Form I-20.

The I-901 SEVIS Fee must be paid before entering the United States.

Applying for a Student Visa

The Form I-20 lists the program start date.

Important requirements include:
- Students may enter the United States no earlier than 30 days before the program start date.
- F-1 and M-1 visas may be issued up to 365 days before the program begins.
- The visa category must match the Form I-20 (F-1 or M-1).
- Students are generally expected to present the original Form I-20 during the visa interview.
- In limited circumstances, a consular officer may accept a copy before the original is received.

Entering the United States

Students should carry the signed original Form I-20 with them when traveling.

Do not pack the Form I-20 in checked luggage.

At the port of entry, a U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) officer may request the Form I-20.

Students may enter the United States up to 30 days before the program start date shown on the Form I-20.

Changing Status in the United States

Individuals already in the United States under another nonimmigrant classification must obtain a Form I-20 before applying to U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) to change status to F or M.

Applying for Student Benefits

The Form I-20 serves as proof of lawful enrollment in a U.S. educational program.

It may be required when applying for benefits available to F and M students, including:
- Driver's licenses.
- Social Security numbers.
- Other student-related benefits.

When to Obtain a New Form I-20
t
A Designated School Official (DSO) may issue a new Form I-20 when:
- The original form is lost, destroyed, or misplaced.
- A travel endorsement is needed.
- The student's SEVIS status changes (for example, from Initial to Active).
- There is a significant change to student information, including:
  - Personal information.
  - Program of study.
  - Optional Practical Training (OPT).
  - Other material updates to the student's record.

Redesigned Form I-20

Beginning July 1, 2016, the redesigned Form I-20 became mandatory for:
- Student visa applications.
- Entry into the United States.
- International travel.
- Applications for nonimmigrant student benefits.

The previous barcode version of Form I-20 is no longer valid.

Designated School Officials (DSOs) were responsible for providing updated paper copies with original signatures to all F-1 and M-1 students.
