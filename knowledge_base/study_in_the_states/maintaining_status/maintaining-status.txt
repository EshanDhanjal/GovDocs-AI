Overview

While studying in the United States, F-1 and M-1 students must maintain their nonimmigrant student status. Maintaining status means fulfilling the purpose for which the student visa was issued and following all regulations associated with that status.

F-1 students attend academic programs, while M-1 students attend vocational programs. Because these programs differ, eligibility for benefits and the length of authorized stay also differ.

Arrival Requirements

Upon arriving in the United States, F-1 and M-1 students must:
- Enter the United States no more than 30 days before the program start date.
- Contact their Designated School Official (DSO) immediately after entering the United States.
- Check in with their DSO no later than the program start date listed on Form I-20.

Academic Requirements

While enrolled, students must:
- Attend all classes.
- Maintain normal academic progress.
- Maintain a full course of study each academic term.
- Contact their DSO immediately if they cannot maintain full-time enrollment.
- Speak with their DSO before dropping a class.
- Request a program extension before the program end date listed on Form I-20 if additional time is needed to complete the program.

Annual Vacation for F-1 Students

F-1 students may qualify for annual vacation after completing at least one full academic year at an SEVP-certified school.

Students must:
- Intend to enroll in the following academic term.
- Follow their school's annual vacation policy.

During an authorized annual vacation, students may enroll in as many, as few, or no courses.

Employment and Practical Training for F-1 Students

F-1 students may work only with proper authorization.

Unauthorized employment is a violation of student status and may result in:
- Immediate departure from the United States.
- Future immigration consequences, including possible inability to re-enter.

Curricular Practical Training (CPT)
- Available during the student's program of study.
- Must be an integral part of the established curriculum.
- Employment must directly relate to the student's major field of study.
- Authorization is provided through the student's DSO according to school policy.

Optional Practical Training (OPT)
- Temporary employment directly related to the student's field of study.
- May occur during or after completion of the academic program.
- Requires proper authorization.

Students should consult their DSO before accepting any employment.

Practical Training for M-1 Students

M-1 students are generally not authorized to work during their program of study.

After completing their vocational program, M-1 students may apply for practical training.

Practical training requires:
- A recommendation from the DSO.
- Authorization from U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS).

Unauthorized employment may result in:
- Immediate departure from the United States.
- Possible future restrictions on re-entering the United States.

Maintaining Status After Program Completion

F-1 Students

After completing the academic program and any authorized practical training:
- Students have a 60-day grace period to depart the United States or take other authorized action.

Possible options include:
- Transfer to another SEVP-certified school.
- Begin a higher educational program.
- Apply for a change of nonimmigrant status (such as H-1B, O, or P status).

M-1 Students

After completing the vocational program:
- Students have a 30-day grace period to depart the United States.
- Students may remain only until the "Admit Until" date shown on Form I-94 unless an extension has been approved by USCIS.

Working with Your Designated School Official (DSO)

The DSO is the primary resource for questions regarding immigration status and maintaining student status.

F-1 students should consult their DSO before:
- Changing their major, program, or degree level.
- Changing educational level.
- Transferring schools.
- Taking a leave of absence.
- Taking a break from school.
- Traveling outside the United States.
- Moving to a new address.
- Requesting a program extension.

M-1 students should consult their DSO before:
- Taking a break from school.
- Traveling outside the United States.
- Moving to a new address.
- Requesting a program extension.

Contacting SEVP

If a DSO cannot resolve a student's question, F-1 and M-1 students may contact the Student and Exchange Visitor Program (SEVP) directly by email at:

sevp@ice.dhs.gov

Students should include:
- A description of their situation.
- Any questions regarding their status or immigration requirements.

SEVP responds to inquiries in the order they are received. Response times vary depending on inquiry volume.
