CONTENT:
Foreign visitors to the United States generally no longer need to complete paper Form I-94 (Arrival/Departure Record) or Form I-94W (Nonimmigrant Visa Waiver Arrival/Departure Record). U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) now automatically collects arrival and departure information from travelers' electronic travel records.

Travelers who need to prove their lawful visitor status to employers, educational institutions, or government agencies can access their arrival and departure records online through the CBP I-94 website or the CBP Link mobile application.

Electronic I-94 at Land Borders
- Travelers entering the United States by land are generally issued electronic I-94 records instead of paper forms.
- CBP encourages travelers who require an I-94 to apply in advance using:
  - The CBP I-94 website.
  - The CBP Link mobile application.
- Travelers using the Visa Waiver Program (VWP) must obtain an approved Electronic System for Travel Authorization (ESTA) before arriving at a U.S. land border.
- VWP travelers are encouraged to obtain their I-94 through the website or mobile app before travel.

Obtaining a Paper Copy
- Travelers who need a paper copy of their I-94 may print it themselves using the "Get Most Recent I-94" feature on the I-94 website or through the CBP Link mobile app.
- Travelers may also request a paper Form I-94 during the inspection process. Such requests are generally handled in a secondary inspection setting.

Using the I-94 for Status Verification
- Travelers who need to verify their immigration status or employment authorization should obtain their I-94 number from the CBP I-94 website or the CBP Link mobile application.

Departure Procedures
Paper I-94 Holders
- Travelers issued a paper Form I-94 should surrender it when departing the United States to:
  - The commercial carrier,
  - The Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA), or
  - U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP).

Electronic I-94 Holders
- Travelers departing by air or sea generally have their departure recorded automatically through carrier manifest information or CBP systems.
- Travelers departing by land may not always have their departure recorded automatically.

Land Departure Recommendations
If a traveler:
- Received an electronic I-94,
- Departs the United States by land,
- Is not a resident of Canada or Mexico, and
- Does not re-enter the United States before the admission expiration date,

CBP recommends keeping evidence of departure, which may include:
- Passport entry stamps.
- Transportation tickets.
- Pay stubs.
- Receipts.
- Other documentation showing departure from the United States.

Travelers may request:
- An entry stamp from the Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA) when entering Canada.
- An entry stamp from Mexico's Instituto Nacional de Migración (INM) when entering Mexico.

Benefits of Electronic Processing
The electronic I-94 system:
- Streamlines the admission process.
- Improves security.
- Reduces administrative costs.
- Saves the federal government an estimated $15.5 million annually.

I-94 Number Format
Beginning in May 2019:
- I-94 numbers became alphanumeric.
- Each I-94 number contains 11 characters.
- Format:
  - Characters 1–9: digits.
  - Character 10: letter.
  - Character 11: digit.
- Previously issued numeric-only I-94 records remain valid until their listed Admit Until Date or the expiration shown on the official I-94 website.
